Privacy Policy

2025 March 29

We appreciate your interest in our Cybersecurity Awareness website. We respect the privacy of those who use our services. This privacy statement describes the data we gather, how we use it, and your rights with relation to it.

Data We Gather

Since users are not required to register on our website, we do not gather personal data such names, email addresses, or passwords. However, we might get a little amount of non-personal data, like:

  • To enhance our content, we use general analytics data (such as page visits and time spent on pages).
  • User engagement using phishing simulation exercises to improve the efficacy of instruction.

How We Use Information Gathered

We utilize the information gathered to:

  • Enhance the user experience and content of websites.
  • Examine how users interact with cybersecurity initiatives.
  • Determine trends in phishing awareness without tracking individuals.

Cookies and Tracking

Cookies and user tracking are not used on this website. We might make use of analytics solutions that are anonymous and don't keep personal information.

Third Party Links

Links to external websites for more resources may be found on our website. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those websites. Before giving any personal information, we advise you to review their privacy policies.

Security of Data

We place a high priority on security and take measures to keep the website safe from unauthorized access and data breaches. However, there aren't several risks because we don't gather sensitive personal data.

Your Rights

Requests for data access, modification, or deletion are unnecessary because we don't gather personal information. Please do not hesitate to contact us if you have privacy concerns.

Changes To This Privacy Statement

This policy may be updated from time to time. This page will be updated to reflect the changes, along with the new effective date.
